01. THE ROOTS

Era: 1960 — 1975
From Determinism to Probabilistic Dreams

Rational Aesthetics

Computational art was born among cards and IBM 7090s. Computers were cold mainframes. Pioneers like Lillian Schwartz didn't use screens—they layered graphics over film and used mechanical plotters.

Max Bense:

The computer as an objective logical system, rejecting subjectivity for "Rational Aesthetics."

Code was a deterministic cage. If you knew the input, you knew the exact output. Every line was a choice, not a probability.

Vera Molnár

"Le 1% de désordre". Molnár introduced organic sensitivity into rigid grids, proving that beauty lies in the slight deviation from the rule.

Harold Cohen

AARON and Symbolic AI. AARON didn't look at the world; it looked at Cohen's internalized rules of composition and form.
